BSL Sentence Structure

BSL has a different sentence structure to both English. As the most basic linguistic analysis, BSL is described as having a topic-comment sign order. Throw the ball being signed – Ball, throw. Using commonly applied linguistic terminology to identify sentence structure, English and BSL have the following sentence structure:

• English: Subject – Verb – Object (The boy rides a bike)

• BSL: Object – Subject – Verb (bike the boy rides)

Smile!

Facial expressions are just one aspect of what are called non-manual features (NMF) within BSL. NMF can make up as much as 50% of the meaning of BSL, so it is important for children to learn facial expressions and the manual sign at the same time.

Dominant Hand

When using BSL, left-handed and right-handed people use different hands, but this must be consistent. Whilst BSL uses a 2-handed signing system, the dominant hand does most of the movement.

True Meaning

In English and Welsh, some words have multiple meanings. When translating from any language into BSL, it is the true meaning that must be signed.

Please keep this important design in mind if you are trying to practise phrases or learning BSL as this will help maximise your communication experience with others.
